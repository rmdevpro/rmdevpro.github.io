LoRAs are powerful in theory but in practice they come with training overhead, weight-hunting, and limited portability across models and hosting environments. I've largely moved away from them in favor of knowledge graphs.

The shift is simple: instead of retraining the model to know something, give the model the right context so it doesn't need to know it. A KG encodes actual relationships — not just semantic proximity — and teaches good and bad behaviors in real time without ever touching base weights.
