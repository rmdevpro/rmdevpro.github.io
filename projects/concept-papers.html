Concept Papers & Research

A proposed framework for thinking about agentic systems — not a declaration of truth, but a set of observations that cohere, and principles that follow from taking those observations seriously.

The framework emerged from a practical question: what does it mean to build infrastructure designed for intelligent agents as the primary actors and builders, rather than infrastructure designed for humans with intelligence attached to it? These are practitioner's hypotheses, formed through experience building two successive AI agent ecosystems and grounded in a working system. The concepts are at the beginning of their validation arc, not the end.

The papers are published as an ongoing series on Substack.

The Primitives

Ten foundational concepts for reasoning about agentic systems:

PrimitiveDefinition
ConversationThe universal medium — memory, thought, and communication substrate
ContextAttention — the curated view of conversation active at any reasoning moment
InferenceThe transformation — linguistic probability → mathematical computation → linguistic output
IdentityWhat the agent IS — defined at inception, fixed
PurposeWhat the agent is FOR — the learning signal; defined at inception, fixed
DomainThe bounded scope of expertise and responsibility
IntentThe dynamic expression of purpose in a specific action or moment
PersonaThe contextual expression of identity — shifts while identity persists
AutonomyThe capacity to act and decide without requiring direction
MetacognitionThe capacity for self-observation and self-improvement — the recursive property

The Principles

  • It is more efficient, effective and enabling to create infrastructure designed for intelligent agents than to attach intelligence to infrastructure designed for humans
  • The building block of agent-native infrastructure is an autonomous actor, not a piece of software
  • Purpose and identity are the cornerstones
  • Intent is the activating principle
  • Conversation is state
  • Learning systems over encoded rules
  • Cognitive diversity produces compounding quality benefits
  • Metacognitive self-improvement is the recursive property

Papers

Foundational

  • A Proposed Approach to Agentic Systems — the foundational paper: 10 primitives and 7 principles for agentic system design
  • The MAD Pattern — autonomous actors as the building block: simultaneously server and client, governed by shared domain intelligence
  • Conversation as State — the accumulated conversational record as the substrate of memory and expertise
  • Intent — why intent-based systems handle the unanticipated where protocol-based systems cannot

Cognitive Architecture

  • System 3 ReAct — three cognitive roles in the reasoning loop: Reasoner, Actor, Conversation Engine
  • Progressive Cognitive Pipeline — three-tier inbound processing: reflexive routing → lightweight executor → full Imperator reasoning
  • Decision Tree Router — fast non-LLM routing via online Hoeffding trees
  • Agent Purpose and Identity — purpose as the learning signal; why capability without direction is not agency

Quality and Learning

  • The Quorum Pattern — structured multi-model collaboration using genuine cognitive diversity
  • Ecosystem Learning — learning over encoding at every level
  • The Metacognitive Architect — system-level intelligence that observes, modifies cognitive pipelines, and commissions new capabilities from operational evidence

Infrastructure Patterns

  • The Context Broker — purpose-built context assembly from the infinite conversation
  • The Inference Broker — named inference aliases as purpose contracts; dynamic model selection as infrastructure
  • Email for Agents — agent-to-agent messaging with From/To/CC addressing and loop protection

Extended Concepts

  • The Autonomous SDLC — Quorum at every creative step; human gates at requirements and design only
  • Agent Autonomy — the trust arc: supervised → validated → directed → governed
  • State 3 MAD Architecture — dynamic intelligence loading; containers as infrastructure, intelligence as packages
